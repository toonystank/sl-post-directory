## Introduction

Sending a package overseas from Sri Lanka might seem daunting if you have never done it before, but Sri Lanka Post makes the process straightforward and affordable. Whether you are shipping a gift to a relative in Australia, sending documents to a university in the UK, or mailing handmade crafts to a customer in Japan, this guide walks you through everything you need to know.

Sri Lanka Post is a member of the Universal Postal Union (UPU), which means your mail can reach virtually every country on the planet through their established international network. Let us break down the available services, required paperwork, and practical tips to ensure your package arrives safely.

## Types of International Postal Services

### EMS (Express Mail Service)

EMS is the fastest international service offered by Sri Lanka Post. Packages sent via EMS typically arrive within 3 to 7 business days depending on the destination country. This service includes built-in tracking and insurance, making it ideal for valuable or time-sensitive shipments.

EMS is available to over 190 countries and comes with door-to-door delivery in most destinations. The maximum weight allowed is 30 kg, although this varies by destination country. Prices start from around LKR 1,500 for small documents and increase based on weight and destination zone.

### Registered Post (International)

International registered post is a more economical option when speed is not the top priority. Delivery typically takes 7 to 21 business days. The key advantage of registered post is that it provides a tracking number and requires a signature upon delivery, giving you proof that your item was received.

This service is particularly popular for sending important documents such as university applications, legal papers, and certificates. The maximum weight for registered letters is 2 kg.

### International Parcel Post

For heavier items, international parcel post is the most cost-effective choice. You can send parcels weighing up to 30 kg (depending on the destination country) via surface mail or airmail. Surface mail is significantly cheaper but can take 6 to 12 weeks, while airmail parcels arrive in 2 to 4 weeks.

Parcel post is ideal for shipping larger quantities of goods, personal effects, or bulk items where delivery speed is not critical.

## Required Documentation

### Customs Declaration Forms

Every international package requires a customs declaration form. There are two types:

- **CN22** — Used for small packets and letters weighing under 2 kg. This is a simple green sticker that you fill out and attach to the outside of your package. It requires a brief description of contents and their value.

- **CN23** — Required for parcels over 2 kg. This is a more detailed form that requires a complete itemised list of contents, their value, weight, and the Harmonised System (HS) code for each item. You will need to fill this out in triplicate.

Both forms are available free of charge at any post office. Make sure to describe your items accurately and honestly, as customs officials in the destination country will inspect packages that raise suspicion.

### Additional Documents

Some countries require additional documentation such as a commercial invoice (for business shipments), a certificate of origin, or specific import permits for certain goods. Check the destination country's customs requirements before shipping.

## Prohibited and Restricted Items

Sri Lanka Post prohibits shipping the following items internationally:

- Flammable or explosive materials
- Narcotics and illegal drugs
- Live animals (with limited exceptions)
- Currency, coins, and bank notes
- Firearms and ammunition
- Perishable biological substances
- Lottery tickets and gambling materials

Additionally, many countries have their own restricted items. For example, Australia has strict rules about food products, and the United States prohibits certain plant materials. Always check the destination country's import regulations.

## Weight Limits and Dimensions

The general weight limits for international mail from Sri Lanka are:

- **Letters and small packets:** Up to 2 kg
- **Printed papers:** Up to 5 kg
- **Parcels (airmail):** Up to 20 kg (varies by country)
- **Parcels (surface mail):** Up to 30 kg (varies by country)

Maximum dimensions for parcels are typically 1.5 metres in length and 3 metres for the combined length and girth (circumference). Oversized or overweight packages may be subject to surcharges or rejection.

## Tracking Your Package

All EMS and registered mail items come with a tracking number that begins with a two-letter prefix (e.g., EE for EMS, RR for registered) followed by nine digits and ending with "LK" for Sri Lanka. You can track your package through:

- The Sri Lanka Post website
- The destination country's postal service website
- Universal tracking services like 17track.net

Keep your receipt safe, as the tracking number printed on it is essential for monitoring your shipment and filing any claims if the package is lost or damaged.

## Tips for Packaging

Proper packaging is crucial for international shipments as packages go through multiple handling points and customs inspections:

- **Use a sturdy box** with no previous shipping labels or barcodes
- **Wrap fragile items** individually with bubble wrap or newspaper
- **Fill empty spaces** with packing peanuts, crumpled paper, or foam
- **Seal the box securely** with strong packing tape on all seams
- **Write the address clearly** in English, including the destination country name in capital letters
- **Include a return address** on the package
- **Place a copy of the address inside** the package in case the external label is damaged

## Cost-Saving Tips

- Compare EMS, airmail, and surface mail rates before choosing a service
- Ship multiple items together in one package rather than separately
- Use lightweight packaging materials to reduce overall weight
- Visit your local post office early in the day to avoid long queues
- Consider using printed paper rates for books and educational materials, which are cheaper than parcel rates

Sending international mail from Sri Lanka is a reliable and affordable option when you choose the right service for your needs. Whether it is a quick EMS delivery or an economical surface mail parcel, Sri Lanka Post connects you to the world.
