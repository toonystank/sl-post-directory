## What Are Postal Codes?

A postal code is a series of numbers assigned to a specific geographic area to help sort and deliver mail efficiently. In Sri Lanka, postal codes are essential for ensuring that your letters and parcels reach their intended destination without delays. Every post office in the country has a unique postal code, and understanding how the system works can save you time and prevent lost mail.

Sri Lanka's postal code system was introduced by the Department of Posts to streamline mail sorting and improve delivery accuracy across the island's extensive postal network, which includes over 4,000 post offices.

## The 5-Digit Format

Sri Lanka uses a **5-digit postal code** system. Unlike some countries that use letters and numbers, Sri Lankan postal codes are entirely numeric, ranging from 00100 to 99999. Each digit in the code carries meaning and helps narrow down the exact location.

The structure works as follows:

- **First 1-2 digits:** Identify the postal division or district
- **Middle digits:** Narrow down to a specific area within the division
- **Last digits:** Identify the individual post office

This hierarchical structure means that by looking at just the first two digits of a postal code, you can immediately tell which part of the country it belongs to.

## How Postal Divisions Map to Codes

Sri Lanka is divided into several postal divisions, each overseen by a regional postmaster. Here is how the first digits map to major regions:

- **00xxx** — Colombo and surrounding areas
- **10xxx** — Greater Colombo suburbs (Piliyandala, Homagama, Avissawella)
- **11xxx-12xxx** — Gampaha and Negombo areas
- **20xxx** — Kandy district
- **21xxx** — Matale area
- **22xxx** — Nuwara Eliya area
- **30xxx** — Kurunegala area
- **40xxx** — Jaffna district
- **41xxx** — Kilinochchi area
- **50xxx** — Badulla area
- **60xxx** — Ratnapura area
- **70xxx** — Anuradhapura area
- **80xxx** — Galle area
- **81xxx** — Matara area
- **90xxx** — Batticaloa area

This system makes it remarkably easy to sort mail. A postal worker seeing a code starting with "20" immediately knows the package is headed to the Kandy region, while "80" means Galle.

## Head Post Offices vs Sub Post Offices

In Sri Lanka's postal hierarchy, there is a clear distinction between different types of post offices, and their postal codes reflect this:

### Head Post Offices

Head post offices (also called main post offices) typically have postal codes ending in **000** or **100**. For example:

- **Colombo Head Post Office:** 00100
- **Kandy Head Post Office:** 20000
- **Galle Head Post Office:** 80000
- **Jaffna Head Post Office:** 40000

These are the primary mail distribution centres for their respective regions and offer the full range of postal services.

### Sub Post Offices

Sub post offices serve smaller areas within a division and have more specific codes. For example, within the Colombo area:

- **Colombo 01 (Fort):** 00100
- **Colombo 02 (Slave Island):** 00200
- **Colombo 03 (Kollupitiya):** 00300
- **Colombo 04 (Bambalapitiya):** 00400
- **Colombo 05 (Havelock Town):** 00500
- **Colombo 06 (Wellawatte):** 00600
- **Colombo 07 (Cinnamon Gardens):** 00700
- **Colombo 10 (Maradana):** 01000
- **Colombo 15 (Modara):** 01500

Each of these sub offices handles mail for its specific neighbourhood, ensuring efficient local delivery.

- **Ask your neighbours:** People in your area will know the local postal code

## Why Postal Codes Matter

Using the correct postal code is not just a formality. It directly impacts your mail in several ways:

- **Faster delivery:** Correctly coded mail is sorted automatically and reaches its destination faster
- **Fewer errors:** Without a postal code, mail workers must manually determine the destination, increasing the chance of misdelivery
- **Required for official purposes:** Many government forms, bank applications, and online services require a valid postal code
- **E-commerce:** When ordering products online, entering the wrong postal code can result in delivery to the wrong location or failed delivery attempts
- **International mail:** Foreign postal services rely heavily on postal codes to route incoming mail to Sri Lanka

## Tips for Using Postal Codes

- Always write the postal code on the last line of the address, before "Sri Lanka" for international mail
- Double-check the code before sending important documents
- If you are unsure about a postal code, search our directory or call the nearest post office
- When filling out online forms, make sure the postal code matches the city or area you have entered
- For Colombo addresses, pay special attention to the specific Colombo number (01 through 15) as each has a different postal code
