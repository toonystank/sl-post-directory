## What Is Registered Mail?

Registered mail is a premium postal service that provides proof of mailing and proof of delivery for your letters and documents. Unlike ordinary mail, which is simply dropped into a postbox and delivered without any tracking, registered mail is recorded at every stage of its journey from the sender to the recipient.

When you send registered mail through Sri Lanka Post, you receive a receipt with a unique tracking number that allows you to monitor your item's progress. Upon delivery, the recipient must sign for the item, creating an official record that your mail was successfully received. This makes registered mail the preferred choice for sending important, valuable, or sensitive correspondence.

## When Should You Use Registered Mail?

Registered mail is essential in several common situations in Sri Lanka:

### Legal and Official Documents

- Court summons and legal notices
- Property deeds and title documents
- Notarised agreements and contracts
- Power of attorney documents
- Government correspondence

### Educational Documents

- University admission applications
- Examination results and certificates
- Scholarship applications
- School transfer certificates
- Thesis submissions

### Financial Documents

- Bank account opening documents
- Insurance claims and policies
- Tax returns and receipts
- Share certificates
- Loan applications

### Personal Important Mail

- Passport applications and renewals
- National Identity Card (NIC) applications
- Valuable photographs or irreplaceable documents
- Formal invitations requiring proof of sending
- Complaint letters where you need evidence of dispatch

## How to Send Registered Mail

Sending registered mail in Sri Lanka is a straightforward process that you can complete at any post office:

### Step 1: Prepare Your Mail

Place your documents or items in a sturdy envelope. Make sure the envelope is properly sealed and the contents are protected. Write the recipient's full name and address clearly, including the correct postal code.

### Step 2: Visit a Post Office

Take your prepared envelope to the nearest post office during operating hours. Most post offices are open from 8:30 AM to 4:30 PM on weekdays and 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM on Saturdays.

### Step 3: Fill Out the Registration Form

At the counter, inform the postal clerk that you want to send your item as registered mail. You will need to fill out a registration form that includes the sender's name and address, the recipient's name and address, and a brief description of the contents.

### Step 4: Pay the Fee

The postal clerk will weigh your item and calculate the total cost, which includes the standard postage plus the registration fee. Payment can be made in cash.

### Step 5: Receive Your Receipt

After payment, you will receive an official receipt with your unique registration number. This receipt is your proof of mailing and contains the tracking number you will need to monitor your item. Keep this receipt safe until you have confirmed delivery.

## Tracking Your Registered Mail

Every registered mail item in Sri Lanka is assigned a unique barcode number that follows a standardised format. The tracking number typically starts with "RR" for domestic registered items, followed by nine digits and ending with "LK" (the country code for Sri Lanka).

You can track your registered mail through several methods:

- **Online tracking** through the Sri Lanka Post website
- **SMS tracking** by texting your tracking number to the designated service number
- **In-person enquiry** at any post office by presenting your receipt
- **Third-party tracking websites** like 17track.net that aggregate postal tracking data

The tracking system will show you key milestones including when your item was accepted, when it arrived at sorting centres, when it was dispatched for delivery, and when it was delivered.

## The Barcode System

Sri Lanka Post uses a modern barcode system for registered mail that integrates with international postal standards. Each registered item receives a unique 13-character identifier:

- **First 2 characters:** Service type (RR for registered)
- **Next 9 characters:** Unique serial number
- **Last 2 characters:** Country code (LK)

This barcode is scanned at every handling point, creating a digital trail that allows both the sender and the postal service to know exactly where the item is at any given time.

## Delivery Confirmation

One of the most important features of registered mail is delivery confirmation. When the postal carrier delivers your registered item:

- The recipient or an authorised person must **sign for the item**
- The delivery date and time are recorded
- If the recipient is not available, a **notice is left** asking them to collect the item from the nearest post office
- Uncollected items are held at the post office for a specified period before being returned to the sender

This signature requirement provides legal proof of delivery, which is why registered mail is accepted as valid proof of communication in Sri Lankan courts.

## What Happens If Your Mail Is Lost?

If your registered mail item is lost or damaged during transit, you have the right to file a compensation claim with Sri Lanka Post. The process involves:

- Presenting your original receipt at the post office
- Filing a written complaint describing the issue
- Providing details of the contents and their value
- Waiting for an investigation, which typically takes 30 to 60 days

Compensation amounts are limited and follow the guidelines set by the Department of Posts. While registered mail does not provide full insurance coverage like EMS, the tracking and proof of posting significantly strengthen your position in any claim.

## Tips for Sending Registered Mail

- **Keep your receipt safe** — it is your only proof of mailing and contains your tracking number
- **Write addresses clearly** in block letters to prevent misdelivery
- **Include a return address** on the envelope so undeliverable items can be sent back to you
- **Use the correct postal code** to speed up sorting and delivery
- **Visit the post office during off-peak hours** (mid-morning on weekdays) to avoid long queues
- **Track your item regularly** after sending to stay informed about its progress
- **Use padded envelopes** for certificates, photographs, and other items that could be damaged
- **Send well in advance** of any deadlines, as registered mail may take 2 to 5 business days for domestic delivery
